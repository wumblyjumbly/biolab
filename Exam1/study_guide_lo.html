Cnidaria

Jellyfish, Corals, Hydra 15 LO Qs
Body Plan (Diploblastic = 2 True Tissue Layers)
Outside → Inside Epidermis (outer, faces away from GVC) → Mesoglea (jelly-like, non-cellular middle) → Gastrodermis (inner, lines the GVC)
Unique Structures
  • Cnidocytes — stinging cells unique to cnidarians
  • Nematocyst — organelle INSIDE a cnidocyte; coils and fires like a harpoon
  • Gastrovascular cavity (GVC / coelenteron) — single internal cavity used for both digestion and distribution

Platyhelminthes

"Flat worms" (platy = flat, helminthes = worms) 17 LO Qs

Phylum-level Features

  • Acoelomate — lack a body cavity entirely; body filled with mesenchyme (parenchyma)
  • 3 germ layers (triploblastic) but NO coelom
  • Bilateral symmetry
  • Incomplete digestive system (except Cestoda which have none) — one opening serves as both mouth AND anus

Tapeworm (Cestoda) Anatomy
  • Scolex — anterior holdfast organ; anchors to host intestine
  • Rostellum — ring of hooks on the scolex
  • Proglottid — each repeated body unit; contains a complete set of reproductive organs
  • Ductus deferens — duct carrying sperm from testis to genital pore

  • Main body cavity: hemocoel (blood-filled; open circulatory system)
  • True coelom is greatly reduced

Squid (Loligo) Anatomy

External Structures

  • 8 arms + 2 tentacles (longer; strike prey)
  • Fins — stability and slow maneuvering
  • Siphon (funnel) — jet propulsion by expelling water
  • Beak — bites and tears prey
  • Pen (gladius) — internal, reduced, chitinous shell

Internal Structures

  • Ink sac — releases dark ink to confuse predators/escape
  • 1 systemic heart + 2 brachial hearts — brachial hearts pump blood to gills
  • Nidamental gland — females only; secretes protective capsule around eggs
  • Digestive gland (hepatopancreas) — secretes digestive enzymes; absorbs nutrients
Clam (Bivalvia) Anatomy
  • Umbo — oldest, most dorsal part of shell; always points anterior
  • Adductor muscles (anterior + posterior) — close the shell valves
  • Incurrent siphon — water + food enter here
  • Excurrent siphon — water exits here
  • Labial palps — near mouth; sort and direct food particles into mouth
  • Foot — muscular; used for burrowing into sediment
  • Gills — gas exchange AND filter feeding (trap food particles)

Segmented Worms 20 LO Qs

Phylum-level Features

  • Body cavity: true coelom (fully lined by mesoderm)
  • Segmentation — body divided into repeated units separated by septa

Oligochaeta + Hirudinea both belong to clade Clitellata (have a clitellum).
Earthworm Anatomy

Digestive Tract

  • Mouth → Crop (food storage) → Gizzard (muscular grinding) → Intestine → Anus
  • Typhlosole — dorsal fold inside intestine; increases surface area for absorption

Reproductive Organs

  • Clitellum — swollen glandular band; forms cocoon around fertilized eggs
  • Seminal vesicles — store and mature sperm before transfer
  • Seminal receptacles — receive and store sperm from mating partner
  • Nephridia — excretory organs (one pair per segment)
Circulatory System
  • Aortic arches — 5 pairs of muscular vessels; pump blood ("hearts")
  • Dorsal blood vessel — carries blood anteriorly (toward head)

Nematoda

Roundworms — Ascaris lumbricoides 12 LO Qs

Key Features

  • Pseudocoel — body cavity NOT fully lined by mesoderm (unlike true coelom which IS)
  • Cuticle — tough outer layer; resists host digestion
  • Ecdysis (molting) — how nematodes grow despite rigid cuticle; this is why they belong to Ecdysozoa
  • Complete digestive system — two separate openings: mouth → pharynx → intestine → anus
Ascaris lumbricoides (Lab Specimen)

General

  • Parasite of — small intestine of mammals
  • Transmission — fecal-oral route (eggs in stool ingested by another host)
  • Sexual dimorphism — female is larger than male

Reproductive Anatomy

  • Female — oviduct carries eggs from ovary
  • Male — ductus deferens carries sperm from testis

Memory Hooks

Legs by group

  • Insects — 6 legs, 3 body regions
  • Arachnids — 8 legs, 2 body regions
  • Decapod crustaceans — 10 legs
  • Centipedes — 1 pair per segment (odd-total)
  • Millipedes — 2 pairs per segment

Shell / cavity trick

  • Porifera — spongocoel (central cavity)
  • Cnidaria — gastrovascular cavity (GVC)
  • Platyhelminthes — no cavity (acoelomate)
  • Nematoda — pseudocoel
  • Annelida / Mollusca — true coelom
  • Mollusca open circ. — hemocoel (blood-filled)

Cnidaria body forms

  • Hydrozoa — polyp AND medusa (hydra = polyp only)
  • Scyphozoa — medusa dominant
  • Anthozoa — polyp ONLY, never medusa

Platyhelminthes gut rule

  • Turbellaria — incomplete gut (1 opening)
  • Trematoda — incomplete gut (1 opening)
  • Cestoda — NO gut at all (absorb through skin)
